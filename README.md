## What's inside

- **Party origins** — where each party came from and what they originally stood for
- **Era-by-era ideology timeline** — how each party's positions shifted across eight historical periods
- **The Great Realignment** — how and why the parties effectively swapped their founding philosophies between 1933 and 1980
- **Rise of partisanship** — when polarization became a deliberate political strategy
- **Interactive electoral map** — trace the geographic realignment from 1860 to 2020
- **Ideology movement charts** — visualize how each party moved on economic, governmental, and social axes
- **Era comparison table** — compare each party's stance on six key issues across eight eras

## Key insight

At their founding, Democrats favored small government and states' rights while Republicans favored strong federal power — nearly the opposite of their modern reputations. The full inversion took roughly 150 years.

## Sources & methodology

This report synthesizes findings from American political history scholarship including works by V.O. Key Jr., Arthur Schlesinger Jr., Heather Cox Richardson, and Thomas Frank, alongside data from the Pew Research Center's American Values Survey and DW-NOMINATE Congressional voting scores.
